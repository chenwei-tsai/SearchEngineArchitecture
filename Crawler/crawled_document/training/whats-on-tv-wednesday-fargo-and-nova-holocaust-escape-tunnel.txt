Last June, archaeologists announced that they had uncovered a forgotten tunnel: a 100-foot-long passageway, five or more feet below the surface, that 80 Jews dug mostly by hand as they tried to escape from a Nazi extermination site in Lithuania during World War II. Here, children of 11 of those men, who evaded what were known as the Ponar killing pits \u2014 where as many as 100,000 people were shot at close range, with their bodies dumped or burned \u2014 recount their fathers\u2019 lives.
