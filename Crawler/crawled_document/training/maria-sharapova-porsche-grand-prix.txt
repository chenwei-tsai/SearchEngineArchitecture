In her second match after a 15-month doping suspension, Sharapova hit nine aces and held serve throughout. She converted three of her four break points. \u201cBeing in the quarterfinals here again is quite special,\u201d said Sharapova, who won the event three times from 2012 to 2014. Less than 24 hours after beating Roberta Vinci in straight sets in her highly anticipated first match, Sharapova said that she \u201cfelt I settled down a little bit.\u201d \u201cI was able to focus on the game,\u201d she added. \u201cI executed a great plan today and I thought I was solid.\u201d Sharapova, who tested positive for the banned substance meldonium at last year\u2019s Australian Open, held off the only break point for Makarova at 5-5, then broke Makarova in the next game to close out the opening set. Sharapova was in control of the second throughout and wrapped up the win with two straight aces to raise her total for the tournament to 20. She improved to 7-0 against Makarova. A five-time Grand Slam champion and former No. 1, Sharapova lost her ranking because of the suspension and entered the event as a wild card. She will play 73rd-ranked Anett Kontaveit, a qualifier who upset fifth-seeded Garbi\u00f1e Muguruza, for a place in the semifinals. In other matches, Anastasija Sevastova beat sixth-seeded Johanna Konta, 6-3, 7-5, to set up a quarterfinal against Simona Halep.
