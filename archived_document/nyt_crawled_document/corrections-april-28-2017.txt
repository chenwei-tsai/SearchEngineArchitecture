{"text": "INTERNATIONAL An article on Thursday about three Americans now being detained in North Korea misstated the age of one of them, Otto F. Warmbier. He is 22, not 21. BUSINESS DAY A listing on the Square Feet page on Wednesday misstated the surname of a landlord broker at 535 Zerega Avenue in the Bronx. He is Josh Gopan, not Gopa. \u2022 A picture caption with an article on the Square Feet page on April 19 about the West Kowloon Cultural District and coming M+ museum of visual culture referred incorrectly to the construction site shown. It is the future location of the West Kowloon Cultural District Art Park, not the M+ museum itself. SPORTS An article on Monday about Yankees catcher Austin Romine misstated, in some copies, the Yankees\u2019 record in games that he had started this season. At the time, they had won eight of 10 games, not nine of 11. \u2022 Because of an editing error, an article on Monday about Armand Duplantis, a top high school pole-vaulter, misstated, in some copies, the world record held by the French pole-vaulter Renaud Lavillenie. It is 20 feet 2 1/2 inches, not 20 feet 1/2 inch. \u2022 An article on Saturday about Latrobe Country Club\u2019s first golf season after Arnold Palmer\u2019s death misstated the surname of a member of Palmer\u2019s regular golf group at the club. He is Marty Newingham, not Newington. THE ARTS A picture caption on Thursday with the continuation of a review of \u201cDer Fliegende Holl\u00e4nder,\u201d at the Metropolitan Opera, misidentified the performer shown with Amber Wagner, who sings the role of Senta. He is Franz-Josef Selig, portraying the character Daland; not AJ Glueckert, who performs the part of Erik. \u2022 A theater review on Thursday about \u201cBandstand,\u201d a new Broadway musical, misstated the number of states in the United States during the period in which the show is set. There were 48 states in 1945, not 50. OBITUARIES An obituary on Tuesday about Robert M. Pirsig, the author of \u201cZen and the Art of Motorcycle Maintenance,\u201d referred incorrectly to a New York Times review of that book.
