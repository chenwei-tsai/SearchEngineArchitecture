{"text": "This year, the La MaMa Moves! Dance Festival is breaking out of its small downtown theaters and onto the internet. The festival, which runs May 18 to June 4 and has a reputation for tapping into timely topics like climate change and gender, will crowdsource choreography for \u201c#Here to Dance,\u201d an online project about human rights. Three local dancemakers \u2014 Annie-B Parson, Raja Feather Kelly and a third choreographer to be announced \u2014 will provide direction for dancers around the world to submit one-minute works in response to human-rights abuses. Those will be shared online, then screened during the festival\u2019s Dancing in the Street Block Party on May 20. Nine world premieres, two United States premieres and one New York premiere are on tap for the festival. Highlights include \u201cMy Memory,\u201d choreographed and performed by the Cambodian dancer Rady Nget, which shares a program with Yoshiko Chuma\u2019s \u201cPI=3.14\u2026Dead End, Hey! All Women!,\u201d a new work featuring a changing cast including Vicky Shick, Jodi Melnick and Irene Hultman. Also on that bill is Orlando Zane Hunger Jr. and Ricarrdo Valentine\u2019s \u201chow to survive a plague,\u201d about the AIDS epidemic. The 2016 Bessie Award-winning choreographer Ephrat Asherie will present \u201cOdeon,\u201d a work set to music by the Brazilian composer Ernesto Nazareth. And Patricia Hoffbauer\u2019s \u201cGetting Away With Murder,\u201d about the history of abuses against women, will have cameos by performers including the choreographer Yvonne Rainer.
