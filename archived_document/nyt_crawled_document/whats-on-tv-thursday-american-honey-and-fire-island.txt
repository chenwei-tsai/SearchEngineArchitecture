An aimless young woman named Star (Sasha Lane, plucked from obscurity on a beach by the film\u2019s director, Andrea Arnold) runs away from an abusive home and takes up with a band of feral teenagers who sell magazine subscriptions door to door. Traveling the heartland in a van \u2014 flirting, arguing, smoking and singing on a trip to nowhere \u2014 they con potential customers with stories about scholarship funds and then hand over their earnings to their supervisor, Krystal (Riley Keough). She rides in a convertible driven by her top earner, the dreamy Jake (Shia LaBeouf), who seemingly keeps her satisfied in other ways as well. Then Star catches his eye. \u201c\u2018American Honey,\u2019 long and messy as it is, is by turns observant and exuberant, and sweet in a way that is both unexpected and organic,\u201d A. O. Scott wrote in The New York Times. THE GAME on Sundance Now. When a defecting K.G.B. agent in 1972 London reveals a plot that could change the course of the Cold War, Daddy (Brian Cox), the head of MI5, assembles a team to investigate \u2014 prompting the Soviets to activate sleeper agents across Britain. But then Joe Lambe (Tom Hughes), Daddy\u2019s prized operative, becomes obsessed with the reappearance of his nemesis and risks letting his quest for revenge jeopardize the operation. The show\u2019s creator, Toby Whithouse, examines a possible traitor in each of the six episodes. \u201cSoviet spies in raincoats and trilby hats may seem a little dated, but in the era of Vladimir Putin, they are not entirely far-fetched,\u201d Alessandra Stanley wrote in The Times when the series debuted in 2014. FIRE ISLAND 8 p.m. on Logo. This unscripted series tunes in as six men move into a beachfront summer share and seek thrills in what one calls \u201cthe gay Disneyland.\u201d The dining is fine, and so is the night life, but like any reality show set in a house, sparks fly \u2014 and sometimes result in explosions. INSIDE THE F.B.I.: NEW YORK 10 p.m. on USA. Dick Wolf and Marc Levin team up on this real-life crime series, which was filmed between September 2015 and September 2016 and embedded with the New York field office of the F.B.I. while attacks were waged in Paris; San Bernardino, Calif.; Brussels; Orlando, Fla.; Nice, France; and the Chelsea neighborhood of Manhattan. \u201cThe threat\u2019s always evolving, so we always have to be evolving,\u201d an agent says. Each of the six episodes follows a different division, including counterterrorism, gang units, cybercrimes and human trafficking. BEERLAND 10 p.m. on Vice.
