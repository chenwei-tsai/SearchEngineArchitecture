{"text": "James Earl Jones is to be honored at this year\u2019s Tony Awards with a special prize for lifetime achievement in theater. The awards administration committee announced Thursday that Mr. Jones, a renowned stage, film and television actor with an unforgettably deep voice, would be given the recognition at the awards ceremony June 11. Mr. Jones, 86, is a theater regular who has appeared in 20 Broadway plays, starting with \u201cSunrise at Campobello\u201d in 1958. He most recently appeared in a revival of \u201cThe Gin Game\u201d in 2015. He has won two Tonys, in 1969 for \u201cThe Great White Hope\u201d (when Clive Barnes, a New York Times critic, wrote, \u201cIf anyone deserves to become that occasional thing, a star overnight, then Mr. Jones deserves no less\u201d) and in 1987 for \u201cFences.\u201d About that performance, Frank Rich, a critic for The Times, wrote, \u201cA black man, a free man, a descendant of slaves, a menial laborer, a father, a husband, a lover \u2014 Mr. Jones\u2019s Troy embraces all the contradictions of being black and male and American in his time.\u201d He has performed in multiple television shows, like \u201cThe Guiding Light\u201d and \u201cRoots,\u201d and many films, like \u201cDr. Strangelove\u201d and \u201cThe Lion King.\u201d But he is best known as the voice of Darth Vader in the \u201cStar Wars\u201d films. The Tony Awards previously announced two other honorary prizes: Baayork Lee, an actress, choreographer and director who founded the National Asian Artists Project and is best known as a member of the original cast of \u201cA Chorus Line,\u201d will receive the Isabelle Stevenson Tony Award, which recognizes volunteerism, while two longtime general managers, Nina Lannan and Alan Wasser, will receive Tony Honors for Excellence in the Theater. The Tony Awards, presented by the Broadway League and the American Theater Wing, honor plays and musicals that are staged on Broadway each season.
