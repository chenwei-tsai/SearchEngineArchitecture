{"text": "The BRIC Celebrate Brooklyn! Festival will be back at the Prospect Park Bandshell this summer with free concerts from Lake Street Dive, Esperanza Spalding and a hometown star, Talib Kweli. For nearly 40 years, thousands have flocked to the bandshell concerts to dance in front of the stage, picnic on the grassy hill or listen from outside the venue\u2019s fence. This year, the season will kick off on June 7 with Lake Street Dive, playing sunny harmony-rich Southern rock. The singer-songwriter Alice Smith, alongside the opening acts Bilal and Kris Bowers, will follow on June 10. As always, the festival\u2019s lineup features stars from around the world. There\u2019s talent from Mexico (Lila Downs, June 29), Jamaica (Chronixx, July 8), Mali (Amadou & Mariam, July 21), and Senegal (Youssou N\u2019Dour, Aug. 12). Andrew Bird will share a bill with Esperanza Spalding on July 28; the pair have previously collaborated. And Talib Kweli, who grew up close by in Park Slope, will join the brass ensemble the Soul Rebels on June 24, along with two other New York City rappers, Pharoahe Monch and Kirk Knight. The 2014 film \u201cSelma\u201d will be screened on Aug. 10, with a live score performed by pianist Jason Moran \u2014 who wrote the film\u2019s music \u2014 and the Wordless Music Orchestra. And Garth Fagan Dance, in its 45th season, will perform on June 30. Several benefit concerts, which sell tickets and help fund the rest of the shows, boast starrier names, including the Shins (June 15) and Sufjan Stevens (July 18).
